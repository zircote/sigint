# Regulatory Analysis: EU AI Act Compliance

**Date**: 2024-Q4
**Analyst**: Legal & Compliance Team
**Jurisdiction**: European Union
**Status**: Assessment Complete

---

## Executive Summary

The EU AI Act introduces significant compliance requirements for AI system providers. Our current product portfolio includes two systems classified as "High-Risk" requiring substantial compliance investment. Timeline for full compliance: Q3 2025. Estimated investment: $1.2-1.8M.

## Compliance Gap Analysis

### High-Risk Requirements Matrix

| Requirement | Article | Product A | Product B | Status |
|-------------|---------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Risk Management System | Art. 9 | Partial | Partial | Gap |
| Data Governance | Art. 10 | Partial | Yes | Partial Gap |
| Technical Documentation | Art. 11 | No | No | Gap |
| Record-Keeping | Art. 12 | Partial | Partial | Gap |
| Transparency | Art. 13 | Partial | No | Gap |
| Human Oversight | Art. 14 | Yes | Partial | Partial Gap |
| Accuracy/Robustness | Art. 15 | Partial | Partial | Gap |
| Conformity Assessment | Art. 43 | No | No | Gap |
| EU Declaration | Art. 47 | No | No | Gap |
| CE Marking | Art. 48 | No | No | Gap |

### Gap Details

#### Risk Management System (Art. 9)
**Current State**: Informal risk assessment process
**Required State**:
- Continuous, iterative risk management
- Documented risk identification and mitigation
- Testing procedures for risk scenarios

**Gap**: Formal system, documentation, testing

#### Technical Documentation (Art. 11)
**Current State**: Engineering documentation only
**Required State**:
- System design and architecture
- Development process description
- Training data specifications
- Validation and testing results
- Performance metrics and limitations

**Gap**: Comprehensive documentation package

#### Transparency (Art. 13)
**Current State**: Basic user documentation
**Required State**:
- Clear indication of AI system
- Capabilities and limitations disclosed
- Intended purpose clearly stated
- Human oversight requirements specified

**Gap**: Structured transparency disclosures

## Recommendations

### Immediate Actions

1. **Classify all AI systems**
   - Complete inventory of AI-powered features
   - Apply risk classification framework
   - Prioritize high-risk systems

2. **Establish governance**
   - Appoint AI Compliance Officer
   - Create cross-functional AI committee
   - Define escalation procedures

3. **Begin documentation**
   - Start technical documentation
   - Implement training data governance
   - Create transparency templates

### Strategic Considerations

1. **Competitive positioning**
   - Early compliance as differentiator
   - Trust and safety messaging
   - Enterprise buyer requirements

2. **Product roadmap impact**
   - Compliance requirements in sprints
   - Human oversight as feature
   - Explainability investments

3. **Geographic strategy**
   - EU compliance enables other markets
   - Anticipate similar regulations globally
   - Build compliance infrastructure once
