# Persona Examples

## B2B SaaS Persona Examples

### The Enterprise Buyer

**Name**: Alex, VP of Engineering

**Profile**
- **Role**: VP of Engineering
- **Company**: Fortune 500 financial services
- **Team Size**: 200+ engineers
- **Experience**: 15 years, 5 in current role
- **Budget Authority**: $500K+ for tools

**Goals**
1. Maintain system reliability (99.9% uptime)
2. Improve developer productivity
3. Reduce technical debt
4. Meet compliance requirements

**Pain Points**
1. Integration complexity with legacy systems
2. Security and compliance approval processes
3. Change management across large teams
4. Vendor lock-in concerns

**Quote**
> "I don't have time to evaluate every shiny new tool. I need proven solutions that my team can actually adopt and that won't create more problems than they solve."

**Buying Behavior**
- **Trigger**: Board pressure, major incident, or strategic initiative
- **Research**: Analyst reports, peer recommendations, reference calls
- **Decision**: Involves security, legal, procurement - 6-12 month cycles
- **Success Metric**: ROI documentation, adoption rates

**Preferred Channels**
- Industry conferences
- Analyst briefings
- Executive dinners
- Reference customers

### The Technical Evaluator

**Name**: Jordan, Senior DevOps Engineer

**Profile**
- **Role**: Senior DevOps Engineer
- **Company**: Mid-market SaaS company
- **Team Size**: 12 engineers
- **Experience**: 8 years
- **Budget Authority**: None direct, strong influence

**Goals**
1. Automate repetitive tasks
2. Reduce on-call burden
3. Keep technology stack modern
4. Build skills and career

**Pain Points**
1. Tool sprawl and context switching
2. Documentation is always outdated
3. Not enough time for proper solutions
4. Pressure to ship vs. do it right

**Quote**
> "Just let me try it. If it works, I'll champion it internally. If it doesn't work in the first 30 minutes, I'm moving on."

**Buying Behavior**
- **Trigger**: Specific problem to solve, saw demo, heard from peer
- **Research**: GitHub, Hacker News, Stack Overflow, hands-on trial
- **Decision**: Recommends to leadership, needs champion
- **Success Metric**: Does it actually work? Is it maintainable?

**Preferred Channels**
- Developer communities
- Technical blogs
- Open source projects
- Hands-on workshops

### The Startup Founder

**Name**: Sam, CEO & Co-founder

**Profile**
- **Role**: CEO & Co-founder
- **Company**: Series A startup, 25 employees
- **Background**: Former product manager
- **Budget Authority**: Everything, but limited funds

**Goals**
1. Find product-market fit
2. Close next funding round
3. Build great team
4. Move faster than competition

**Pain Points**
1. Too many priorities, not enough time
2. Limited resources for everything
3. Making decisions with incomplete data
4. Investor expectations vs. reality

**Quote**
> "I need tools that help us punch above our weight. But I can't afford enterprise pricing or enterprise complexity."

**Buying Behavior**
- **Trigger**: Immediate problem, investor recommendation, competitor using
- **Research**: Network, Twitter, quick web search
- **Decision**: Fast - days not months
- **Success Metric**: Immediate impact, team adoption

**Preferred Channels**
- Twitter/X
- Founder networks
- Investor recommendations
- Product Hunt

## Consumer Persona Examples

### The Busy Professional

**Name**: Taylor

**Demographics**
- **Age**: 32
- **Role**: Marketing Manager
- **Location**: Urban, apartment
- **Income**: $85K
- **Tech Comfort**: High

**Goals**
1. Advance career without burning out
2. Maintain work-life balance
3. Stay current with industry trends
4. Build professional network

**Pain Points**
1. Information overload
2. Not enough hours in the day
3. Hard to disconnect from work
4. FOMO on opportunities

**Quote**
> "I need things that save me time, not add to my to-do list. If it takes more than 2 minutes to figure out, I'm out."

**Behavior**
- Heavy mobile user
- Pays for premium to save time
- Influenced by professional peers
- Values efficiency over features

### The Price-Conscious Parent

**Name**: Morgan

**Demographics**
- **Age**: 41
- **Role**: Parent of 2, works from home
- **Location**: Suburban
- **Income**: $120K household
- **Tech Comfort**: Medium

**Goals**
1. Provide for family
2. Manage household efficiently
3. Save for future
4. Keep kids safe and educated

**Pain Points**
1. Subscription fatigue
2. Coordinating family schedules
3. Balancing screen time
4. Unexpected expenses

**Quote**
> "Is this really worth it? I need to see the value before I commit, and I hate surprises on my credit card."

**Behavior**
- Comparison shops extensively
- Reads reviews carefully
- Values free trials
- Loyal once trust established

## Persona Development Tips

### Do
- Base on real interview data
- Include direct quotes
- Make specific and actionable
- Update as you learn more
- Validate with sales/support

### Don't
- Make up details
- Create too many personas (3-5 max)
- Include irrelevant demographics
- Treat as static documents
- Forget anti-personas (who you don't serve)
