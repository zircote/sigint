## Source Evaluation Criteria

### Reliability Assessment
| Factor | High | Medium | Low |
|--------|------|--------|-----|
| Methodology | Documented | Partial | Unknown |
| Sample size | Large, representative | Adequate | Small/biased |
| Recency | <6 months | 6-18 months | >18 months |
| Independence | Third-party | Mixed | Vendor-sponsored |
| Track record | Proven accuracy | Mixed | Unproven |

### Citation Format
```markdown
**Source**: [Name]
**Date**: [Publication date]
**Methodology**: [How data was collected]
**Limitations**: [Known caveats]
**Reliability**: High/Medium/Low
```

## Search Strategies

### For Market Size
1. "[Industry] market size [year]"
2. "[Industry] TAM SAM"
3. "[Industry] market forecast CAGR"
4. "site:gartner.com [industry] market"

### For Competitor Intelligence
1. "[Company] revenue"
2. "[Company] market share"
3. "[Company] funding round"
4. "[Company] vs [alternative]"
5. "site:[competitor.com] pricing"

### For Trends
1. "[Technology] adoption rate"
2. "[Industry] trends [year]"
3. "[Topic] Gartner hype cycle"
4. Google Trends: compare terms over time

### For Customer Insights
1. "[Product category] reviews G2"
2. "[Product] vs [alternative] Reddit"
3. "[Industry] buyer survey"

## Data Quality Checklist

- [ ] Source is reputable and cited
- [ ] Methodology is documented or inferable
- [ ] Data is recent enough for purpose
- [ ] Sample size is adequate
- [ ] Cross-validated with second source
- [ ] Limitations are noted
- [ ] Currency and geography specified
- [ ] Growth rates vs. absolute numbers clear
